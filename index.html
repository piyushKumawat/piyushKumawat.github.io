Hello!

I am a second-year PhD student at the Paul G. Allen School of Computer Science & Engineering, University of Washington. I work with Prof. Tim Althoff in the Behavioral Data Science and Natural Language Processing group. My research interests are in Natural Language Processing, Conversational AI, and Computational Social Science. In particular, I am passionate about problems related to conversational intelligence and NLP for mental health. My current research builds computational techniques for encouraging empathic conversations in text-based mental health support.

Previously, I was a Research Fellow at Microsoft Research India, where I worked with Dr. Monojit Choudhury. I have a dual degree (B.Tech. + M.Tech.) in computer science from the Indian Institute of Technology, Kharagpur. For my bachelor's and master's thesis projects, I was advised by Prof. Niloy Ganguly. I have also done research internships at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign and Adobe Research Lab, India. For more details, refer to my Resume or drop me an email!
